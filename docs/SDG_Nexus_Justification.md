# Scientific Justification: The SDG 3 + SDG 16 Nexus

This document provides the theoretical and humanitarian rationale for combining **Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-being)** and **Sustainable Development Goal 16 (Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions)** within the Myanmar Conflict Observatory analytical framework.

## 1. The Conflict-Health Nexus
In active conflict environments, health and peace are interdependent. The project argues that a medical crisis is often the delayed result of a justice crisis. 

*   **SDG 3 (Health)** focuses on physical outcomes: infrastructure, medical personnel, and disease prevention.
*   **SDG 16 (Peace & Justice)** focuses on behavioral drivers: violence, looting, arrests, and the collapse of rule of law.

By combining these, the model moves from **retrospective body-counting** to **systemic risk assessment**.

## 2. Theoretical Pillars of the Integrated Approach

### A. Lead Indicators of Health Collapse
Events traditionally categorized under SDG 16 (e.g., the arrest of a medic or the looting of a village) are **lead indicators** for SDG 3 failures. 
*   **Example:** When a village is looted (SDG 16 violation), the theft of food and medical supplies directly causes malnutrition and prevents the treatment of chronic illness (SDG 3 crisis).

### B. The 'Medical Neutrality' Mandate
International Humanitarian Law requires "Safe Zones" for medical delivery. 
*   **Rationale:** The threat of arbitrary arrest or abduction (SDG 16) creates a "climate of fear." Even if a hospital building is intact, patients and doctors will not utilize it if the security environment has collapsed. Therefore, tracking SDG 16 violations is essential to understanding the **functional failure** of the health system.

### C. Structural Violence as a Health Determinant
The framework adopts the concept of **Structural Violence**. It acknowledges that the systematic destruction of schools, homes, and civil administration (SDG 16) creates long-term displacement. Displacement is the primary driver of secondary health emergencies, including cholera outbreaks and maternal mortality.

## 3. Policy Alignment (The UN 'Triple Nexus')
This integrated approach aligns with the **UN Humanitarian-Development-Peace Nexus**. This policy mandates that aid organizations must address the root causes of instability (Peace/Justice) while delivering life-saving services (Health).

## 4. Conclusion for the Research Paper
In the context of Myanmar's asymmetric warfare, analyzing SDG 3 and SDG 16 in isolation provides an incomplete picture. The **Vulnerability Score** integrates these two goals to identify "Red Zones" where the total humanitarian environment has collapsed, providing a more accurate target for aid prioritization than fatality counts alone.
