# Semantic Actor Taxonomy Guide

This guide provides a detailed explanation of the six primary actor categories used in the **Myanmar Conflict Observatory (MCO)**. Due to the extreme fragmentation of armed groups in Myanmar (over 400 unique identifiers), this taxonomy is essential for meaningful spatiotemporal analysis and regional risk modeling.

---

## 1. State Forces (Tatmadaw & Police)
**Core Definition:** The official security apparatus of the State Administration Council (SAC).
*   **Composition:** Primarily the **Myanmar Armed Forces (Tatmadaw)**, including the Army, Air Force, and Navy. It also includes the **Myanmar Police Force** and specialized units like the Border Guard Forces (BGF) that are under direct military command.
*   **Tactical Profile:** Characterized by conventional warfare capabilities, including heavy artillery, airstrikes (MiG-29, Yak-130), and large-scale "clearance operations."
*   **Observatory Role:** Acts as the primary state-aligned kinetic actor in the majority of engagements.

## 2. Resistance (PDF / LDF / Urban Guerrillas)
**Core Definition:** Anti-coup forces that emerged in response to the February 2021 military takeover.
*   **Composition:** 
    *   **People's Defense Forces (PDF):** Armed wings officially recognized by the National Unity Government (NUG).
    *   **Local Defense Forces (LDF):** Independent, village-based units that may or may not coordinate with the NUG.
    *   **Urban Guerrillas:** Small, clandestine cells operating in major cities like Yangon and Mandalay.
*   **Objective:** The total removal of military influence from Myanmar's governance and the restoration of democracy.
*   **Tactical Profile:** Asymmetric warfare, IED (Improvised Explosive Device) attacks, drone strikes, and "hit-and-run" ambushes.

## 3. Ethnic Armed Organizations (EAOs)
**Core Definition:** Long-standing revolutionary organizations representing Myanmar's ethnic minority nationalities.
*   **Composition:** Groups such as the **KNU** (Karen), **KIA** (Kachin), **AA** (Arakan), **TNLA** (Ta'ang), and **MNDAA** (Kokang). Most have existed for decades (some since 1948).
*   **Objective:** Historically focused on ethnic self-determination, federalism, and territorial autonomy.
*   **Hybridization:** Since 2021, many EAOs have become "Force Multipliers" for the Resistance, providing training, weapons, and joint operational command in what the research paper identifies as a **Hybridization Trend**.

## 4. Pro-Junta Militias (Pyu Saw Htee / Proxy Forces)
**Core Definition:** Local armed groups formed or supported by the military to counter the Resistance at the village level.
*   **Composition:** Most notably the **Pyu Saw Htee** militias, often composed of military veterans, ultranationalists, or coerced villagers.
*   **Objective:** To provide local security for SAC administration, serve as guides for military columns, and disrupt Resistance logistics.
*   **Observatory Role:** Their presence often correlates with high levels of communal violence and arson in rural heartlands (e.g., Sagaing and Magway).

## 5. Civilians (Non-Combatants)
**Core Definition:** Individuals who are not members of any organized armed group or state security force.
*   **Targeting Logic:** In the MCO, "Civilians" are tracked as a distinct actor category to measure the **humanitarian cost of conflict**.
*   **Key Indicators:** Includes victims of indiscriminate shelling, airstrikes on IDP (Internally Displaced Persons) camps, and "collateral damage" during battles.
*   **Significance:** Tracking civilian-involved events is critical for monitoring violations of International Humanitarian Law (IHL) and documenting the human rights impact of the conflict.

## 6. Protesters & Civil Society
**Core Definition:** Actors engaged in non-violent resistance, demonstrations, or civil disobedience.
*   **Context:** While kinetic battles now dominate the landscape, protests remain a vital signal of political sentiment.
*   **Observatory Role:** Used to track the transition from civil disobedience (2021) to armed struggle (2022-present). Events in this category often involve military crackdowns on peaceful assemblies.

## 7. Unidentified Armed Groups
**Core Definition:** Kinetic incidents where the perpetrator remains unknown or has not claimed responsibility.
*   **Context:** This is common in targeted assassinations (e.g., of local administrators), unclaimed IED/bomb attacks, and nighttime raids where witnesses cannot distinguish between various armed factions.
*   **Observatory Role:** While "Unidentified," these events are vital for tracking the **general level of insecurity** and clandestine warfare that characterizes the current state of conflict.

---

### Data Note: The "Semantic Filter"
When you use the **Myanmar Conflict Observatory**, our backend logic automatically maps hundreds of local names (e.g., *"Monywa PDF"* or *"Light Infantry Division 33"*) into these six high-level categories. This allows for clear, aggregated visualizations of the macro-level conflict dynamics across Myanmar.
